Research Focus

Where lava meets
landscape — and beyond.

Dissertation

Fagradalsfjall Lava Flow Emplacement

Documenting how basaltic flows from Iceland's 2021–2023 eruptions were emplaced and how their surface morphology records thermal and rheological history — a template for interpreting ancient lava fields elsewhere in the solar system.

Planetary Analogs

Volcanic Surfaces on Mars & Venus

Translating terrestrial lava flow models to planetary contexts. Contributing to the NASA VERITAS Discovery mission, applying high-resolution surface mapping methods to Venusian volcanism.

Methods

Remote Sensing & Photogrammetry

Integrating drone-based SfM photogrammetry, DEM analysis, and radar datasets (Magellan, VERITAS, FSAR) with field mapping to characterize lava flow morphology at scales from centimeters to kilometers.

Modeling

Flow Dynamics & Surface Disruption

Developing Python-based models of lava flow surface disruption incorporating slope mechanics, eddy dissipation, and thermal effects (Glaze et al. 2014; Hon et al. 1994) to predict morphologic outcomes from rheological inputs.

About

The science of
flowing rock.

I'm a PhD candidate in Geosciences at the University of Arizona, with joint affiliation in Lunar and Planetary Sciences, advised by Dr. Christopher Hamilton. My research sits at the intersection of volcanology, remote sensing, and planetary science — using Earth's most active lava fields to build frameworks for reading volcanic histories across the solar system.

As an NSF GRFP Fellow and member of the NASA VERITAS Discovery mission team, I work at the boundary between terrestrial hazard science and planetary exploration. Understanding how lava flows behave and what signatures they leave in a landscape has direct implications both for communities living near volcanoes and for scientists trying to reconstruct the volcanic histories of Mars and Venus.

Outside the lab and field, I'm a trail and marathon runner, rock climber, and drone pilot. I find the same quality of attention in all of it — moving carefully through terrain you don't entirely control.
